Using relative you have to know where the element would have been relative to its neighbours - that is its 'zero' position, and all changes are relative to that.

All changes that are made to the elements may then affect the 'normal position' and can have a huge effect on the relative position. For instance if elements that are inline get wider, one may spill over into the line below - moving from right of one element to way left of it, and below another element. And it's relative position moves with it.
